Great design isn't easy. We're not all super-geniouses who can come up with the best idea off the cuff. You don't need to wait for that flash of inspiration though - you can find real solutions by asking the people who know best - the users.

To create a great design, I start by asking questions. That’s how I identify the real problems. Next, I work with my other team members to brainstorm options that we can refine to the best solutions.

Methods

Iterative Design Cycle

The life-cycle of a product goes research, design, testing, design, testing, design, testing, design...

Workshop Facilitation

Workshops can harness the collective know-how and creativity of the whole team. When you need to get everyone brainstorming and brewing up new ideas, you need a great facilitator to bring it together.

Rapid Prototyping

Sometimes you need to try out a great idea as soon as possible. You might not have time for a fully functioning prototype. You might need a sketch or a powerpoint or a clickable axure prototype - what's important is getting something ready when you need it.

Think Aloud Interview

Think aloud interviews are the bread and butter of user research. Find out what the users are thinking when they don't click the right button!

Survey & Data Analysis

When you need to make a decision based on quantitative data, you need a good survey and a good analysis.

Coffee Appreciation

If you're making that nice ethopian in a french press how will you ever taste the tart, fruity flavor notes?
